3D Printing Connectors - Chandelier

March 2016, Computer Integrated Manufacturing

The goal of this project was to 3D print connector pieces that would be able to hold together CDs, straws, and/or popsicle sticks. This was an open-ended project that had very few constraints. My partner and I could create anything that we wanted, as long as it consisted of the materials listed above, with the addition of rubber bands, the material was less than 2 cubic inches, and that we used at least eight connectors in our project. We decided to make an original and aesthetically pleasing product. After a lot of brainstorming, my partner and I decided that we could best do this by creating a chandelier. We made one central holder for the popsicle sticks, so two sticks went one way while two sticks went the other way. Using Autodesk Inventor, we modeled and printed ten connectors that use 1.183 cubic inches of material. There is a small hook on the top of this piece so that a rubber band can be strung through it, creating a hangar that can hold a wall hook for the top so that the chandelier can be put onto a wall. One straw fits in between each popsicle stick, so we used four straws total. On the top of each straw there is a clamp that holds the popsicle sticks and straws together so that they are held at a uniform level. On the bottom of the straw, there is a connector piece that is a medallion with a peg sticking upwards and the peg uses tension to grip the straw. This piece lets a CD rest on top of the medallion and the straw can go through the middle of the CD. LEDs can be added to the bottom of the CD to add a cool light effect, especially since the light is reflected off of the bottom of the CD. The design itself is fairly simple, but we had to come up with many different ideas on how to hold all of the items and give enough strength to the straws to be able to be held up while also holding onto the small CDs. If you want the CD's to be hung at different levels, you can cut the straws to length. My partner and I were really happy with how our project turned out, it looked nice and we were able to change the design to fix the challenges we had. I learned that there are simple solutions to hard problems, but in order to make these solutions work you need to be able to think outside of the box and have accurately measured products. The measurements of our printed pieces were true to design. On our CD holder, the total diameter was exactly the same as the model. The holes on the inside were supposed to be .157 inches and turned out to be .154 inches, which is very close. For our wall hook, the diameter was supposed to be 1 inch and it was really .998. The extrusions, however, for both the base and the hook itself were exactly what they were supposed to be. The connector piece for the popsicle stick holder was also almost exact to the design. The height was exactly the same. The width of both of the sides were supposed to be .875 inches but came out to .880, .005 inches off. The holes on the inside were fairly accurate, they were between .007 to .12 inches off of the design. All of our pieces worked together, so these small measurement differences did not make a difference in the final product.

MIT Android App Inventor: No Texting While Driving

January 2016, Computer Science and Software Engineering

The goal of this project was to create a working Android application using MIT App Inventor. My partner and I decided to make a no texting and driving application because we believed that it would be a useful and relevant app, especially for teenagers. Our app is simple and designed to not distract the driver if they recieve a message while driving and need to immediately send a response. When a text is received, it defaults to being read aloud but there is an option to turn this off for privacy. The driver then has three options for how to respond to the message: three predetermined messages that send with the press of a button, a speech-to-text option, and typing a message. The predetermined messages are "I'm driving and will respond later", "I will arrive shortly", and "I just left and am on my way". If the driver decides to type a response, a message shows up for the passenger to type it so that the driver is reminded that they should not be texting and driving. There is also an option that allows for the driver to press a button to automatically call back the number that texted them. The .apk file of the app can be downloaded onto any Android device for use. This project was my first experience in making an application and also allowed for me to work on my problem solving and logic skills.

Delivery System

December 2015 - January 2016, Computer Integrated Manufacturing

For this project, my team and I had to create a delivery system that would transport objects to predetermined places. We had to have a working program and prototype and an Autodesk Inventor model of our delivery system. My team created a delivery system that was a flat car with a claw on the side and a bump switch and potentiometer. The claw was situated on the side of the delivery system so that it would be able to pick up objects but not obstruct the path of the car. The delivery system began when the potentiometer was spun and stopped when the bump switch was pressed. The claw picks up an object, moves for a certain amount of time, drops the object, picks up another object, and repeats indefinitely. We also had to calculate the cost of implementing these delivery systems into large warehouses. Our delivery system came out to be $139, but when the parts are bought in bulk the price decreases. To be implemented into warehouses, the delivery systems would need to be built at different heights. Since the claw is at a fixed height, the delivery systems would also have to be at different heights so that they would be able to pick up objects from different level shelves in a warehouse. I was responsible for making the program and making modifications on the prototype.

Platform Design

March 2015

I had to create a free-standing platform that would be able to hold a spa and drop 29 inches into a deck. This platform needed to support a dry weight of 847 lbs, filled weight of 3,975 lbs (375 gallons of water), and a fully loaded weight of 5,200 lbs (6 adults). The top load capacity was 100 lb/sqft. I needed to draw up a site plan that showed not only the install location but also the location of the septic and leach lines on the property to ensure the spa could not be contaminated by the septic system. The final product was created using cement supports on the bottom with four beams held together by trusses, column bases, and joist hangars. I also needed to find the type of wood and supports needed to be able to support the fully loaded weight and have the structure be safe. After receiving the permits to build, my family and I spent a weekend building the platform itself.

Entrepreneur Project: Bike and Ped Button

May 2015, Principles of Engineering

The objective of this project was to create an invention or innovation that was completely original. I created a new walk button for cross walks. This new button, the Bike & Ped Button, has two buttons instead of the preexisting one. The first button would be the exact same as what exists today in regards to allotting time for a person to cross. The second button would be different and specifically geared towards runners and bikers. This button allots less time for a person to cross the street. By allowing a shorter amount of time for a person to cross, cars do not need to idle their cars in the cross walk for an unnecessarily long amount of time, thus reducing their emissions. This solution could reduce approximately one million pounds of CO2 emissions per day. My presentation won the award of "Best Presentation" both visually and orally in the Final Showcase for all Entrepreneur Projects in my school.

Solar and Hydrogen Vehicle

November 2014, Principles of Engineering

The objective of this project was to design, create, and test a prototype that utilizes both solar and hydrogen fuel cells to power a vehicle. The car would have to be wired in parallel or series so that the user would be able to use one or two solar cells, one or two hydrogen cells, or one solar cell and one hydrogen cell to power the motor of the car and move the car for at least on meter. My team built a lightweight vehicle so that the car would be able to travel as far as possible. As project manager, I made sure that my team was meeting deadlines and creating the best vehicle possible. I was also in charge of wiring the vehicle and the calculations. I wired the two hydrogen cells in parallel and the two solar cells in series. After we ran the trials for the vehicles and their different cell configurations, my team had to decide which of the energy sources was most efficient. We concluded that the hydrogen cell was more efficient and effective in moving the prototype since one hydrogen cell was able to power the motor to run the car whereas two solar cells were not able to do this. After we documented our findings, each team member was responsible for creating their own Net Logo GUI that accurately depicted how each car ran with each energy source and wiring configuration. This project taught me about different energy sources and their benefits along with electrical wiring and how to properly create circuits.

Mechanical Winch System

October 2014, Principles of Engineering

The project objective was to harness electrical energy as mechanical energy. This was to be done by creating a closed circuit that powered a mechanical winch system. The winch system was capable of lifting at least a 100 gram weight thirty centimeters and it was turned on and off with a switch. My team decided to use a pulley to act as the winch due to its stability and simple construction. My specific role in the project was to create a simulation of the winch in Net Logo. The program simulates the weight being lifted by the mechanical winch. In the program, the winch size, angular speed of the winch, mass of the weight, and voltage of the power supply is capable of being altered to simulate different scenarios the mechanical winch could encounter. In this project, I was introduced to code and how to overcome challenges in writing code during this project. The code can be seen here if NetLogo is installed onto your computer. This project also helped to show me the advantages of dividing aspects of the project among all members of the team for success along with the importance of collaborating between aspects of the project to perfect the final product.

Project Compound

September 2014, Principles of Engineering

In this project, my team and I designed four simple machines that worked as one cohesive unit to work as one machine that could accomplish a task, pouring a cup of water. The simple machines we used were an axle driving wheel, a pulley, inclined plane, and a first class lever. By spinning the axle, the wheel was powered to pull a box containing a ball by a pulley. The ball, once the box was high enough to tilt, went down an inclined plane and dropped to hit a first class lever. The lever's opposite end lifted to tip a cup on an axle, which then poured water into an output cup, ready for the consumer to drink. This project taught me how to make multiple machines work as one and how to use different ideas and modify the design for success. Along with this, it gave me a deeper understanding of how machines work and how to use them to complete and overall goal.
