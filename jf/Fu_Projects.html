Projects

MIT App Inventor

Course: CSE (Computer Science/Software Engineering)

Type of project: Pair Programming

Length: Around 2 weeks (September 27 - October 14, 2016)

Goal: Create and design a mobile app or game using MIT App Inventor

In this project, my partner and I created a revamped version of a simple Magic 8 Ball Application. We added material design, color effects, tapping to speak and different screens. The biggest challenge of this project was pair programming and collaboration, as I found myself doing the majority of the work and debugging.

Scratch Project

Course: CSE (Computer Science/Software Engineering)

Type of project: Pair Programming

Length: Around 2 weeks (August 30 - September 16, 2016)

Goal: Create an interactive game or story using Scratch

In this project, my partner and I decided to create an interactive story/game about the unlucky fate of Harambe. This project is what made me realize how much more effective pair programming is. We used our strengths to our advantages. My partner did a great job coming up with new ideas and scenes, while I was familiar in the Scratch language and great at working with variables, scenes, and settings. In this project, our biggest problem was circumventing the bugs that we faced as a result of the many variables, scripts, and scenes we had implemented in our game/interactive story.
