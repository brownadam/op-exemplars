Image Artist - Python

2017, Computer Science/Software Engineering (CSE)

The Image Artist project was done by me and my partner on Cloud 9. The objective of the project was to manipulate multiple images in the same way using an algorithm programmed in Python. My team had to manipulate the images appropiately to cater to our client, which we decided would be an animal shelter called Furry Friends Shelter. Throughout the duration of the project, we documented our progress and satisfied a set of criteria. I contributed by coding half of the algorithm and updating the documentation. The actual code can be accessed by clicking the link on the title page of the documentation. An example of a before and after image can be seen on the left. All final images can be found here.

Space Invaders - MIT App Inventor

2016, Computer Science/Software Engineering (CSE)

Space Invaders is an Android app created on MIT App Inventor by me and my partner. The objective of the project was to follow a basic tutorial and make our own stylistic changes to it based on a set of criteria. My partner and I decided to make changes to alter the difficulty and complexity of the game in a time frame of two weeks. I contributed by coding and documenting the application's development. The app can be downloaded (link in documentation) and tested on any Android device.

Dog Mario - Scratch

2016, Computer Science/Software Engineering (CSE)

Dog Mario is a simple platform game created through Scratch, a basic programming language. The objective of the project was to design, code, and document a game fulfilling a certain criteria. Over a period of two weeks my partner and I coded and tested in small increments to ensure the functionality of the game. We developed a beta and final version of the game, both of which underwent peer review. I contributed by coding about half of the game as well as keeping a thorough documentation of the project's development.

unSalt Me - Dublin Entrepreneur Project

2016, Principles of Engineering (POE)

As a part of a four-member team I created unSalt Me, a portable desalination bottle, as a solution to water scarcity for the Dublin Entrepreneur Project. We had a time frame of about 2 weeks to brainstorm, create a solution, and prepare a 3 minute presentation as well as an electronic documentation. My contributions included research, documentation, and construction of the physical model. My research dealt with the need for our product, such as statistics on water scarcity. Afterwards, I compiled this information onto the electronic documentation and the presentation, focusing mainly on customization. The project was nominated for the showcase and recieved the Ability to Solve a Global Probem award.
