Online Portfolio Project

Teacher Resources

Creating the Foundation

All students start by creating two foundational pages. This is done to ensure that every student have the ability to complete the Online Portfolio Project. However, for the students that are more advanced, this does not limit their creative freedom. Once students have shown they have completed the foundational pages with their information, they are given full creative freedom to complete their Portfolios. For those students that struggle, the tips table is there to help them.

Checking Progress

Once a majority of students have completed the two foundational pages, screen captures of the code used for those pages is given out. All students are encouraged to check their work. Students are asked if they can see any similarities or differences between their code and the one provided. They are also not asked or encouraged to make the two codes match. The only thing that is strongly encouraged is to see if their code is laid out in a similar manner to make grading easier.

Project Files

We then go over the below documents with the students and talk about all of the requirements of the Online Portfolio. Students Exemplars are not shown to them until they have all made some style decisions of their own. I do not want to limit their creativity. Exemplars are mainly used to show how some students kept the original template from the foundational pages or created their own. The last two exemplars are used for the more advanced students, or as motivation to continue learning. Once students have completed the project and they have received feedback from the teacher they are able to use Github to deploy their websites.
