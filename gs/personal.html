Student

  My formal education began in Canada, where I spent the first 12 years of my life until moving to California. I lived near Toronto where I was born and raised, and here I completed most of my elementary studies. My interests in technology was sparked during this time, especially with my technology oriented family.

  Three years later, I'm now studying as a junior in Dublin High. Through my education, my informal goal is to improve the world through improvements in technology, specifically Computer Science. Here at Dublin High I am taking many CS oriented classes, and invest heavily in mathematics and science. For instance, this year I am taking AP Computer Science Applications, as well as doubling in AP Calculus AB, and AP Statistics. Every year at Dublin high has been heavily concentrated in the rigorous STEM fields, and every year I've taken the most prestigious engineering courses.

   Much of my introduction and inspiration in pursuing technology comes from my projects and classes. I have lots of experience with website building, VEX robotics engineering, and C++ game development. I have many personal projects that use the Cinder C++ graphical library, and have many years of experience with this program. I am also now a Varsity tennis player on my high school team. I have had brief experience helping children play tennis, mostly in small groups of young (ages ~10). In my robotics team, we have won several awards and qualified to states. My primary role is to program the robot and design mechanisms, but I often participate in the building process. My team and I also designed and prototyped a device to dispense medications at a certain time. This project, DoseMaster, won first place in the Alameda County Science Fair and also won the "Build award" at the school's local engineering fair.

   Most of my free time consists of project building and tennis. My most advanced project so far is a two dimensional fully Cinder C++ built simulation of the VEX robotics game: "In the Zone". This simulation has fully integrated and optimized physics built in and many other features such as an autonomous enemy robot and re-run code parser and producer. I have also had experience with technology assistance in my own high school. During our lunch and prep periods, I am stationed in the main library helping other students in the school with technology related issues.

Engineer

   Some of my work includes robotics code, including velocity measurers and PID (Proportional Integral Derivative) controllers, as well as some other personal projects. Those include --but are not limited to-- my tennis simulation, this website, other basic games, and most importantly, my new 2017 Robotics simulation. Although I won't go too in depth of my crown jewel right now, further information can be found in my projects page or here. Anyways, I invest many hours during the week for my own personal projects which is really where I spend most of my free time now. This may sound tedious and turning to some, but I truly enjoy further developing my little projects and making them something larger. For example, my entire Simulation has been publicly available on GitHub for the past few months, and I have never had a week without thinking about it or working on it since. Most of my smaller projects I would give up on, and would halt the progress, since I must've found something better. However, my Vex robotics simulation and tennis simulations have been worked on so much that I can actually develop them to real world applications that include machine learning, two dimensional physics, and graphics design. All this is mostly what I spend my time on, and I am always proud of my work and progress.

  I began my robotics career in Freshman year, when I joined the Dublin High Robotics club. Although I was already interested in Computer Science and software development, I hadn't yet put those skills to use realistically. At the end of Freshman year I was quickly admitted into the main competition teams, competing in the VEX "Starstruck" game of 2016. This was 5327D. Our team consisted of myself and seven other members who have been long lasting friends ever since. My primary role on 5327D was the lead programmer, ensuring the autonomous period and driver controls worked perfectly and efficiently. Our robot was initially extremely successful as we frequently made it to finalists and even were tourney champs for a few of the tournaments we attended. We won several awards but our primary focus was on having the best robot and winning. 5327D, along with most of the other Gael Force teams qualified to the Vex states championships for north California, however we stopped there and did not advance to worlds.

   This year I am on another robotics team, 5327C, led by one of my peers, and a good friend from my last team, Christy K. Since day one, we've been hard at work designing the best robot for the new VEX game "In the Zone", and now being an "elder" I was able to take more part in the team's inner decisions and major changes. Similarly to my last team I was lead software developer and it was my responsibility to get the robot working exactly as the driver would want, and make sure the autonomous portion of the match would be effective and reliable. I also dabble in the video production of our team, website design, notebook records, and external projects. So far our team was the first 5327 team of five to qualify to states through the most prestigious Bellarmine Excellence award given to the "best robot in the competition", in terms of design, presentation, numbers, and ability. I can honestly take quite a bit of credit for the award since the judges were awed by some of the external projects I demonstrated and will further delineate below.

Athlete

I learned that taking many advanced classes is not as simple as it sounds, and it takes a whole lot of dedication, effort, patience, and respect to succeed in those mentioned classes.

   I now play at least once every three days, but sometimes have to make compromises due to workload. I've been on my high school's Varsity Tennis team since Freshman year, and am always practicing, improving, and pushing my limits. I now play in a small class at ClubSport that has helped me along the years ever since moving to California.
